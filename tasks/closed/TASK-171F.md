# TASK-171F — Israeli supplement market coverage + maintenance economics

**CLOSED 2026-06-03.** Deliverable: `02_products/supplements/market_coverage_analysis_v1.md`.
Triangulated 3 measurable lenses (no fabricated sales figure): L1 real Super-Pharm catalog
(7,429 SKUs → 366 oral supplements), L2 Google-Trends Hebrew demand (19 actives), L3 global
category-share benchmark. Rejected `il_gov_data.imported_foods` as a supplement lens (food registry).

**Answers:** (1) current-5 ≈ **15–25%** (Med); (2) next 10 = multivitamin, probiotics, vit C, zinc,
iron, collagen, calcium, folic acid, B12, B-complex — 6 easy, **top-2 (multivit, probiotics) structurally
hard**; (3) at 15 actives ≈ **40–55%** (Med), curve flattens, does NOT reach 70%; (4) structurally-hard
shelf ≈ **20–30%** blend-adjusted — **the biggest sellers are the hardest to score**; (5) maintenance
**1–2 h/yr settled vs 4–6 h contested**; ≈24–40 h/yr @15 dossiers, ≈80–115 h/yr @50.
**Bottom line: bounded widen to ~15 tractable actives (~doubles coverage, low maintenance); the next 20–30
pts need the deferred blend/CFU/standardization machinery — a separate, explicitly-budgeted strategic call.
Don't pause; don't widen blindly into blends expecting clean-active economics.** Decision-support only; no
scoring/engine/roadmap change.
