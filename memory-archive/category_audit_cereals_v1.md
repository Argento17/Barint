**Why:** Cereals was selected as the highest-stress category — activates the most distortions simultaneously (002, 003, 004, 009, 010), has the most ambiguous lens assignments (granola, children's, protein cereals), and has the most endemic marketing divergence patterns (whole grain, natural, fortification claims).

**How to apply:** Cereals cannot launch until all four gaps below are resolved. Two require Tom authorization per Operating Model decision rights.

## Verdict: C — No, governance gaps remain

Four gaps prevent a B verdict:

### Gap 1 — Children's Cereal Operational Definition (CRITICAL)
No criterion exists anywhere in the governance stack for what constitutes a "children's product." Without it, Lens 3 / developmental pool separation cannot be applied. Requires Tom authorization. Proposal: primary packaging targeting children + pediatric serving size norms as joint trigger.

### Gap 2 — Category-Level Distortion Disclosure Format (CRITICAL)
DISTORTION-004 (fortification) is category-critical in cereals — affects nearly every product. Existing distortion registry documents individual product-level findings. No format exists for category-wide distortion disclosure on the shelf page. This gap applies to all future categories where a distortion reaches endemic prevalence. Constitution v1 Article VI must be amended to include this format.

### Gap 3 — Granola Sub-Category Pool Ruling (SIGNIFICANT)
Granola cannot be assigned to a comparison lens without a manual governance decision. As a processed confection with wellness marketing, it is simultaneously Lens 1 (default), Lens 2 (fiber/protein claim support), and potentially uncategorizable. Ruling required: treat as standing precedent for sub-category pool exceptions going forward.

### Gap 4 — Whole Grain Marketing Divergence Threshold (SIGNIFICANT)
"Whole grain" is the most prevalent claim in cereals. The Marketing Divergence Finding standard requires a quantitative threshold to be triggered. No threshold defined in any governance document. Without it, the finding cannot be applied to the most common marketing pattern in the category. Requires Tom authorization. Proposal: ≥51% whole grain flour by weight in ingredient list as threshold.

### Gap 5 — Serving Size Normalization (MODERATE)
Manufacturers define their own serving sizes (25g–55g range). Without normalization, per-serving nutritional comparisons are distorted before scoring begins. Not a launch blocker but affects comparison integrity.
