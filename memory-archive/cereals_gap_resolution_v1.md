## Four Resolutions

### Resolution 1 — Children's Product Definition (Gap 1, CRITICAL)
Added: Constitution v1, Section 2.8
Rule: 2-of-4 indicator required. Indicators: D1 animated/child-directed packaging, D2 child-targeting name/language, D3 pediatric serving size (≤25g cereals, ≤30g snacks — calibrate per category), D4 developmental claim.
Anti-gaming: classification history is retained. Anti-immunity applies: classification cannot improve score.

### Resolution 2 — Endemic Distortion Protocol (Gap 2, CRITICAL)
Added: Constitution v1, Section 6.4 + checklist criterion C5
Trigger: distortion affecting ≥50% of products activates category-level shelf notice (not per-product).
Format: "CATEGORY NOTE — [Name]" + one sentence on the limitation + prevalence language + what is/isn't captured.
DISTORTION-004 (fortification) is the inaugural endemic distortion — activates for cereals and plant-based milk.

### Resolution 3 — Granola Sub-Category Pool (Gap 3, SIGNIFICANT)
Added: Constitution v1, Section 2.9 (Architectural Divergence Sub-Category Rule)
Trigger: any two of three proxy indicators concurrently: NOVA≥3 vs category median NOVA 1-2; added sugar ≥10g/100g vs median ≤5g; fat ≥10g/100g from added fats vs median <5g.
Boundary cases default to the separate pool (conservative). Granola is the standing precedent.
Cross-pool comparisons permitted with purpose divergence disclosure. Anti-immunity applies.

### Resolution 4 — Whole Grain Threshold (Gap 4, SIGNIFICANT)
Added: Guardrails v2, Section 5.2.1 (Claim Threshold Reference Table)
Composition claim: ≥30% whole grain, detection = first-ingredient position test.
Dominant claim: ≥51% (FDA/WGC standard), detection = whole grain flour first AND no refined flour of same grain.
Keto: ≤5g net carbs/100g. High protein (general): ≥20% calories or ≥15g/100g solid.
Claims not in table → Finding cannot be issued until threshold documented (triggers D6 checklist requirement).
