## Nine Components

1. **Core Interpretation Principle** — 4 versions: short ("ברי מתאר. לא ממליץ."), long, internal (what the engine does/does not encode), consumer-facing
2. **Interpretation Boundaries** — 58 CAN/CANNOT pairs across 10 domains: fermentation, grain/flour, fiber, protein, sugar, seeds, data, marketing claims, manufacturer intent, scoring
3. **Three-Layer Model** — Observation → Analytical Interpretation → Consumer Framing. Layer containment rule: consumer framing cannot exceed interpretation layer; interpretation cannot exceed observation layer.
4. **Uncertainty Governance** — 5-level ladder (full/mixed/partial/insufficient/conflicting), standard phrasings, UX examples, article examples, conservative interpretation rule
5. **Category Independence** — Universal principles (alignment, transparency, evidence hierarchy, conservative interpretation, confidence disclosure) vs. category-native signals. Onboarding protocol for new categories. Bread logic must not leak into yogurt/cereals.
6. **Anti-Drift Tests** — 10 questions applied before any publication. Failure = rewrite, not adjust.
7. **Framework Invisibility Rules** — Internal systems (FQC, GSS, archetypes, BSIP) never surface in consumer output. Sophistication gradient by context. Simplicity test.
8. **Commercial & Legal Humility** — 4 wording variants (public/internal/product-page/article). Intent rule: never attribute manufacturer motivation. No-recommendation rule covers explicit, implicit, and emotional forms.
9. **Long-Term Identity** — Investigative, restrained, trusted, evidence-based, calm, transparent, intellectually honest. Never: activist, influencer, fear-based, wellness guru, recommendation engine, pseudo-scientific.
