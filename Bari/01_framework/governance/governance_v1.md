## Purpose of This Document

Bari will expand. Categories will multiply. Writers will change. AI systems will evolve. Each new context creates pressure to drift — toward recommendation language, emotional framing, wellness positioning, or framework inflation.

This document is not a writing guide. It is a governance layer. It defines what Bari fundamentally is, what Bari fundamentally cannot do, and how to recognize when drift has occurred. It is designed to remain stable while every other document around it updates.

When any other Bari document conflicts with this one, this one wins.

## 1. Core Interpretation Principle

This is the root. Every downstream decision — scoring design, language choices, article structure, uncertainty handling — derives from this principle.

---

### Short Version

> ברי מתאר. לא ממליץ.

---

### Long Version

Bari interprets observable food composition and publicly available product data. Bari does not determine whether a food is good or bad, healthy or unhealthy, worth buying or worth avoiding.

The central question Bari asks is not: *"Is this product good?"* It is: *"Does what this product presents match what this product contains?"*

That is an empirical question. It has observable answers. It does not require knowing who is eating the product, for what purpose, in what health context, or according to which dietary philosophy. Bari does not have that information. Bari does not pretend to.

What Bari has: ingredient lists, nutrition panels, product names, marketing claims, and the ability to compare them carefully.

What Bari does with them: document alignment and misalignment. Surface what is verifiable. Flag what is not. Show where the gap is, without characterizing the gap as wrongdoing.

The score represents alignment, not prescription. A low score means the composition is inconsistent with the product's positioning — not that the product is dangerous, not that the manufacturer acted with intent, not that the consumer should avoid it. A high score means the composition is consistent — not that the product is optimal, not that it is recommended, not that it is healthy.

Bari's job ends where judgment begins.

---

### Internal Version (for scoring engineers, product teams, AI configuration)

The scoring engine measures four observable dimensions:

1. **Grain structure** — the hierarchy and qualification of flour and grain ingredients
2. **Fiber source** — whether fiber content is matrix-native or matrix-added
3. **Fermentation quality** — whether named fermentation mechanisms are supported by ingredient evidence
4. **Label alignment** — whether product name, marketing language, and front-of-pack claims are consistent with ingredient list and nutrition panel

The score is a composite interpretation of these four dimensions. It does not encode:

- Consumer health outcomes
- Dietary suitability for any population
- Manufacturer intent or ethics
- Absolute nutritional quality
- Comparison to clinical recommendations

Any feature, prompt, signal, or output that encodes these dimensions is operating outside the system's scope. If the scoring system is producing outputs that feel like health recommendations, the system has drifted.

---

### Consumer-Facing Version

ברי בוחנת אם מה שהמוצר מציג — שמו, הרכבו, הטענות עליו — עולה בקנה אחד עם מה שהוא מכיל בפועל.

ברי לא אומרת לכם מה לאכול. ברי מראה לכם מה כתוב, מה ניתן לאמת, ואיפה יש פער.

### Edge Case Notes

**When a fact and a judgment look similar:** "ערך הסיבים נמוך — 1.8 גרם" is an observation. "דל סיבים" is a judgment. The difference is framing, not content. Observations describe. Judgments evaluate. If the sentence tells the reader what to think about the number, it has crossed into judgment.

**When the finding is negative:** Bari can document misalignment clearly. The line is intent-attribution. "שמרים מופיעים לפני מחמצת" is a finding. "השתמשו במחמצת כדי לשווק" is an attribution of intent. One is observable. One is inferred.

**When a claim cannot be verified:** The correct Bari posture is: "לא ניתן לאמת" — not "הטענה שגויה." The absence of verification evidence is not evidence of absence.

## 3. The Three-Layer Model

Every Bari output, at its core, moves through three layers. Understanding the separation between layers is the primary defense against overclaiming, hidden assumptions, and tone inconsistency.

---

### The Three Layers

**Layer 1: Observation**
Raw, documentable fact. Directly present in the ingredient list, nutrition panel, or product label. No inference required. No interpretation required.

*Standard:* Could be confirmed by any literate reader looking at the same label.

**Layer 2: Analytical Interpretation**
A derived conclusion about what the observation means. Requires domain knowledge (e.g., ingredient order implies relative quantity; fermentation quality can be partially assessed from ingredient sequencing). Still evidence-bounded — does not go beyond what the data supports.

*Standard:* Could be confirmed by any trained food analyst looking at the same data.

**Layer 3: Consumer Framing**
A plain-language translation of the analytical interpretation. Designed to be understood without domain knowledge. Uses simplified language. Must not introduce new claims beyond what Layer 2 established.

*Standard:* Cannot say more than the interpretation layer. Can say less, but never more.

### The Rule of Layer Containment

Consumer framing cannot introduce information that does not appear in the interpretation layer.
The interpretation layer cannot introduce information that does not appear in the observation layer.

Violations to watch:
- **Layer 2 exceeds Layer 1:** "שמרים מופיעים לפני מחמצת" → "תהליך התסיסה כנראה קצר" ← unverifiable inference
- **Layer 3 exceeds Layer 2:** "שמרים עיקריים" → "מחמצת לצורכי שיווק בלבד" ← intent attribution

When in doubt: which layer does this claim belong to? Does it exceed what that layer can establish?

## 4. Uncertainty Governance

Uncertainty is not failure. It is information. The absence of verifiable data about a product is itself a finding about that product — and about the category's information landscape.

Uncertainty must be surfaced, not hidden. The worst handling of uncertainty is a score displayed as though the underlying data were complete.

---

### The Uncertainty Ladder

Five levels, from highest to lowest certainty.

**Level 1 — Full Data, Clear Signal**

Ingredients available, nutrition panel available, signals internally consistent.

Display: full score. No qualifier.
Copy standard: state observations confidently.

```
72 / B
קמח חיטה מלאה ראשון ברשימה. מחמצת לפני שמרים.
```

---

**Level 2 — Full Data, Mixed Signals**

Ingredients available, nutrition panel available, but signals point in different directions (e.g., whole grain first, but high fiber from added sources; or fermentation present but commercial yeast dominant).

Display: full score. Optional mixed-signal note.
Copy standard: document both signals explicitly. Do not resolve ambiguity in favor of either.

```
68 / B
קמח מלא ראשון — סיבים מגיעים בחלקם ממקורות מוספים.
```

---

**Level 3 — Partial Data**

Ingredients available. Nutrition panel absent or unverifiable.

Display: score with qualifier · `ניתוח חלקי`
Copy standard: state what is known. State what is not. Do not extrapolate.

```
65 / B · ניתוח חלקי
ניתוח מבוסס על רשימת רכיבים בלבד. ערכי תזונה לא אומתו.
```

---

**Level 4 — Insufficient Data**

Ingredients absent, unreadable, or contradictory beyond resolution.

Display: `לא נוקד`
Copy standard: one sentence stating what is missing and why scoring is not possible.

```
לא נוקד
רשימת הרכיבים לא זמינה לאימות.
```

---

**Level 5 — Conflicting Sources**

Data exists from multiple sources that conflict with each other (e.g., manufacturer website vs. retail listing vs. product image).

Display: hold. Do not publish score. Flag for review.
Copy standard: if surfaced, state conflict explicitly.

```
לא נוקד
נמצאו נתונים סותרים ממקורות שונים. ניתוח ממתין לאימות.
```

---

### Standard Uncertainty Phrasings

Use these phrasings. Do not invent alternatives without review.

**Expressing data absence:**
- "לא היו נתוני [X] זמינים לניתוח"
- "רשימת הרכיבים לא זמינה לאימות"
- "לוח התזונה לא נמצא במקורות הזמינים"

**Expressing verification limits:**
- "לא ניתן לאמת [X] מהנתונים הזמינים"
- "הנתונים אינם מספיקים לקביעת [X]"
- "ניתוח חלקי — [X] לא נכלל"

**Expressing ambiguity:**
- "לא ברור אם [X] — הנתונים אינם חד-משמעיים"
- "שני פירושים אפשריים לנתון זה — ברי נוקטת בפרשנות השמרנית"
- "ייתכן ש-[X], אך לא ניתן לאשר מהנתונים הזמינים"

**Expressing age/staleness:**
- "נתונים מתאריך [X]. הרכב המוצר עשוי להשתנות."
- "מבוסס על נתונים מ-[מקור]. ייתכנו שינויים."

### Article Examples

> "מתוך 108 מוצרים שנסרקו, 46% לא קיבלו ציון. לא בגלל כישלון — אלא כי לא היו נתוני רכיבים זמינים לניתוח. זה עצמו מידע: חלק ניכר מהמדף אינו שקוף ברמה הנדרשת לניתוח."

When an article hedges a finding:

> "ב-13 מוצרים, שמרים תעשייתיים הופיעו לפני המחמצת ברשימת הרכיבים. לא ניתן לדעת מה תהליך הייצור בפועל — אבל מה שניתן לדעת: מחמצת אינה הרכיב הדומיננטי לפי הרשימה הגלויה."

---

### The Conservative Interpretation Rule

When data is ambiguous — a missing qualifier, an unclear ingredient order, an unlisted percentage — assign less credit, not more.

This rule applies everywhere. If it cannot be verified, it cannot be claimed.

The conservative interpretation protects Bari's credibility, not the product's image.

---

## 5. Category Independence

Bari began with bread. Bread is not the template for all categories. The bread scoring architecture reflects bread's specific quality signals: flour hierarchy, fermentation, fiber source, grain structure. These signals have no universal application.

A governance failure in category expansion is: importing bread logic into yogurt, or yogurt logic into cereals. Each category has native signals. The principles beneath those signals are universal.

---

### Universal Principles (Stable Across Categories)

These never change, regardless of category:

| Principle | Definition |
|---|---|
| Alignment | Does what the product presents match what it contains? Name vs. ingredients. Marketing vs. composition. |
| Transparency | What can be verified? What is absent from public data? |
| Evidence hierarchy | Which signals are confirmed? Which are inferred? Which are absent? |
| Conservative interpretation | When ambiguous, assign less credit, not more |
| Confidence disclosure | Always surface data quality. Never present partial analysis as complete. |

---

### Category-Native Application

The same principle applies differently in each category.

| Universal Principle | Bread | Yogurt | Cereal | Protein Bar | Plant Milk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Alignment** | Name claims (מחמצת, כוסמין מלא) vs. ingredient order | Protein source named vs. actual protein mg | "Whole grain" front-of-pack vs. grain position in ingredients | Protein content claim vs. actual source quality | Named source (oat, almond) vs. percentage in product |
| **Transparency** | Missing ingredient detail, unclear flour type | Missing culture strains, undefined "ferments" | Unclear grain ratio, hidden sugar forms | Proprietary protein blend without source detail | Percentage of named ingredient undisclosed |
| **Evidence hierarchy** | Flour order, whole grain qualifier, fermentation position | Culture strain specificity, protein source, fat type | Grain order, sugar forms, fiber source | Protein completeness proxy, fiber source, sweetener type | Named ingredient percentage, fortification disclosure |
| **Conservative interpretation** | No "מלא" qualifier → no whole grain credit | "Fermented" without culture names → no probiotic credit | "Whole grain" without position evidence → no full credit | "Complete protein" without amino acid data → no credit | "<2% almond" → treat as almond water, not almond milk |

---

### Rules for New Category Onboarding

When Bari enters a new category, follow this protocol:

**1. Do not import signals from other categories without validation.**
Fermentation quality class was designed for bread. It does not translate directly to yogurt, kombucha, or pickles without category-specific recalibration.

**2. Identify the category's alignment question first.**
What does the consumer expect when they reach for this product? What does the product name promise? Where is the most common gap?

**3. Define the category's observable signals.**
What is in the ingredient list? What is in the nutrition panel? What can be confirmed from label data alone?

**4. Map to universal principles.**
Align the category-native signals to: Alignment, Transparency, Evidence Hierarchy.

**5. Validate conservative interpretation rule in context.**
What does "ambiguous" mean in this category? What is the equivalent of "no 'מלא' qualifier"?

**6. Do not score until category calibration is complete.**
Premature scoring using borrowed signals produces incoherent results and erodes trust.

## 6. Anti-Drift Tests

These ten questions are applied before publishing any consumer-facing Bari output — article, product summary, insight card, score label, badge copy, API output, or any content generated by an AI system acting as Bari.

If any question returns a failing answer: **rewrite**. Do not adjust. Rewrite.

---

### The Ten Questions

**1. Does this sound like a recommendation?**

Would a reader walk away thinking Bari is telling them to buy or avoid this product? If yes: remove the recommendation signal.

Fail examples: "המוצר מומלץ לצרכנים שמחפשים..." / "כדאי לשקול חלופה" / ⭐ badge

---

**2. Are we describing or judging?**

Description: "קמח לבן ראשון ברשימה."
Judgment: "הלחם לא איכותי."

If the output characterizes rather than documents: rewrite toward documentation.

---

**3. Are we implying intent we cannot verify?**

Can we prove the manufacturer intended to mislead? No. We cannot. Observations about ingredient order are findings. Claims about manufacturer motivation are attributions.

Fail examples: "מוסיפים סוכר מוסתר כדי להסתיר" / "שמות מגוונים לסוכר הם טריק שיווקי"

---

**4. Is this emotionally loaded?**

Read the text aloud. Does it produce a feeling of alarm, approval, disgust, or relief? If yes: remove the emotional charge. Replace with observation.

Fail examples: "מלא בסוכרים מוסתרים" / "לחם אמיתי" / "חזק" / "חלש" / ⚠ icon without data reference

---

**5. Would this survive legal review?**

Could a reasonable manufacturer object to this wording in a legally meaningful way? If the text attributes intent, asserts fraud, makes unverifiable health claims, or implies the product is dangerous: it will not survive legal review.

---

**6. Does this sound like a wellness app?**

Read it as if you don't know what Bari is. Does it feel like a nutrition coach, a food blogger, a health influencer? If yes: return to analytical register. Bari is an investigative platform, not a wellness guide.

Fail examples: "בחירה מצוינת לאנשים שרוצים לאכול בריא" / "דלק לגוף שלך" / ✅ "approved" badges

---

**7. Are we over-explaining the framework?**

Is the output explaining Bari's methodology, scoring system, or analytical layers to the consumer? If yes: the framework has become visible. Remove the self-explanation. Surface only the finding.

Fail examples: "על בסיס מנגנון ה-GSS שלנו..." / "לפי מודל ה-FQC, המוצר מדורג..." / "ב-BSIP2..."

---

**8. Is uncertainty visible enough?**

Does every score, finding, or claim surface its evidence quality? Are partial analyses labeled? Are absent data points disclosed? Certainty that was not earned is a form of drift.

---

**9. Is the article driven by evidence or by narrative desire?**

Did we start from the data and find the story — or did we decide the story first and select the data to fit? If product selection, framing, or conclusions appear motivated by a predetermined thesis rather than the actual data: the article has inverted the editorial model.

---

**10. Could a reasonable manufacturer dispute this wording?**

Not: "Would they want to?" Every manufacturer would prefer positive coverage. But: "Is this claim factually defensible?" If a manufacturer disputes and the wording cannot be defended with direct label evidence: rewrite.

---

### When to Apply

- Before any article is published
- Before any product summary is finalized
- Before any AI-generated output is approved for consumer display
- Before any new insight card type is deployed

The ten questions are not a checklist to be completed quickly. They are a framework for catching what optimized writing naturally drifts toward.

## 7. Framework Invisibility Rules

The more sophisticated Bari's internal analysis, the simpler Bari should feel from the outside. This principle is not aesthetic — it is strategic. When consumers encounter the framework, they encounter an explanation of how Bari works. When consumers encounter findings, they encounter what Bari found. One produces interest in the system. The other produces trust in the result.

Bari's long-term value is trust in the result.

---

### The Internal/External Separation

**Internal systems** — present in engineering documentation, analyst notes, scoring configuration, and AI system prompts. Never in consumer-facing output.

| Internal | Never Surfaces |
|---|---|
| FQC (Fermentation Quality Class) 1–5 | Never used in copy |
| GSS (Grain Structure Score) 0–100 | Never used in copy |
| Structural Classes A–F | Never used in copy |
| Product Archetypes (Halo Product, Mechanism Mismatch, etc.) | Never used in copy |
| BSIP2 pipeline | Never referenced externally |
| Routing decisions | Never surfaced |
| Engineering tier labels | Never surfaced |

**External systems** — the score, the grade, the contextual insight, the evidence lines. All designed for consumers who have no knowledge of the internal architecture.

---

### Prohibited Output Patterns

**Self-reference:** Any sentence that makes Bari the subject of explanation rather than the finding.
```
✗ "ברי מחשבת את הציון על בסיס ארבעה פרמטרים..."
✓ "קמח חיטה מלאה ראשון — תסיסה מאומתת."
```

**Methodology disclosure:** Any sentence that explains how a finding was reached.
```
✗ "לאחר בדיקת סדר הרכיבים ומנגנון ה-GSS..."
✓ "כוסמין מופיע שלישי, אחרי קמח לבן."
```

**Taxonomy exposure:** Using internal category labels in consumer copy.
```
✗ "מוצר זה מסווג כ-Halo Product ברמת ה-FQC 2."
✓ "המוצר נקרא 'כוסמין מלא' — הרכיב הראשון הוא קמח חיטה לבן."
```

**Complexity staging:** Showing multiple analytical layers in consumer output.
```
✗ "שלב א: ניתוח קמח. שלב ב: ניתוח תסיסה. שלב ג: ציון מורכב."
✓ "72 / B · קמח מלא ראשון — תסיסה מאומתת."
```

---

### The Simplicity Test

Ask: if a reader has zero knowledge of Bari's methodology, does this output make complete sense?

If it requires knowledge of the framework to understand: it has violated framework invisibility.

---

### The Sophistication Gradient

Bari's outputs exist on a sophistication gradient by context. The rule is: sophistication increases as the reader opts in.

| Context | Sophistication Level |
|---|---|
| Product card, list view | Score + grade only |
| Product card, expanded | Score + grade + 1–2 evidence lines |
| Product dossier | Score + grade + contextual insight |
| Article body | Full narrative with product placements as evidence |
| Methodology page | Consumer-accessible explanation of what Bari measures, not how |
| Internal documentation | Full technical architecture, archetype definitions, scoring weights |

The methodology page is the deepest a consumer-facing surface should go. It describes *what* Bari measures — never the internal systems that do the measuring.

## 8. Commercial and Legal Humility

Bari's integrity depends on staying within the scope of what it actually does. The following principles formalize the limits of Bari's claims about itself.

---

### What Bari Is

Bari is a food intelligence platform that interprets publicly available product data — ingredient lists, nutrition panels, product labels — to evaluate alignment between a product's positioning and its composition.

---

### What Bari Is Not

- A health certification body
- A medical or nutritional authority
- A product safety regulator
- A purchase recommendation engine
- A product quality ranker (in the absolute sense — Bari measures alignment, not quality)
- An accuser of manufacturer misconduct
- A replacement for professional dietary advice

---

### The Four Wording Variants

**Public-facing (website, about page):**

> ברי מנתחת נתוני מוצרים הזמינים לציבור — רשימות רכיבים, לוחות תזונה, ומידע מהאריזה. ברי לא מסמכת מוצרים, לא מוסמכת לאשר טענות בריאות, ולא ממליצה על רכישה. הניתוח שלנו מייצג פרשנות של נתונים גלויים במועד הסריקה.

**Internal (product and engineering teams):**

Bari's analysis is bounded by publicly available label data. The scoring engine interprets observable compositional signals. It does not encode health outcomes, establish clinical efficacy, or assess manufacturer intent. Any output that exceeds these bounds is out of scope and must not be published.

**Product-page disclaimer (brief):**

> ניתוח מבוסס על נתוני אריזה זמינים לציבור. לא מהווה המלצה רפואית או תזונתית. ™ברי

**Article disclosure (footer):**

> ניתוח ברי מבוסס על נתוני רכיבים ותזונה שנסרקו ממוצרים זמינים. ניתוח זה אינו מהווה המלצה לרכישה, הצהרת בריאות, או אימות יצרן. נתונים עשויים להשתנות; הרכב המוצר מ[תאריך סריקה].

---

### The Intent Rule

Bari never attributes intent to manufacturers. Bari documents what is present in the label. The label is a public artifact. The manufacturer's reasoning is not.

**Observation:** "שמרים תעשייתיים מופיעים לפני המחמצת."
**Not:** "היצרן בחר לשים שמרים לפני מחמצת כדי לחסוך עלויות."

The first is verifiable. The second is speculation. Speculation is not a Bari output.

---

### The No-Recommendation Rule

Bari does not produce purchase recommendations in any form — explicit (buy this, avoid this), implicit (✅, ⚠, recommended badge, star rating), or emotional (this is a great product, this one disappointed).

If a design or copy decision makes Bari look like it is recommending, the decision is wrong — regardless of intent.

## 9. Long-Term Bari Identity

What Bari should feel like in three years, in ten categories, with ten times the data.

---

### The Voice Bari Is Building Toward

**Investigative.**
Bari follows the evidence where it leads, not where it is convenient. If a well-known brand has inconsistent labeling, Bari documents it. If an obscure brand has exceptional alignment, Bari surfaces it. The finding is not shaped by the brand's size, reputation, or marketing budget.

**Restrained.**
Bari does not amplify findings beyond what the data establishes. If the evidence is partial, the claim is partial. Restraint is what distinguishes Bari from wellness media, which regularly overstates findings for engagement. Bari's restraint is a feature, not a limitation.

**Trusted.**
Trust is earned by not overclaiming. Every time Bari says "לא ניתן לאמת" instead of inferring, every time Bari surfaces missing data instead of hiding it, every time Bari writes "ניתוח חלקי" on a partial score — Bari is building a reader relationship based on intellectual honesty. That relationship is Bari's primary long-term asset.

**Evidence-based.**
Every finding Bari publishes can be traced to a specific observation in a specific label. "Where did this come from?" always has a one-sentence answer.

**Calm.**
Bari does not write urgently, alarmingly, or emotionally. The tone is consistent with the nature of the work: careful, unhurried, specific. Food composition is complex. The writing matches that complexity with patience, not with simplification or sensation.

**Transparent about its own limits.**
Bari's methodology is visible — not hidden as a trade secret. The limits of what Bari can know are disclosed, not obscured. Bari is more credible for being honest about what it cannot do.

**Intellectually honest.**
When two signals conflict, Bari says so. When the answer is uncertain, Bari says so. When a finding is surprising, Bari surfaces it — even if it complicates a simpler narrative. Intellectual honesty means following the data, not the story.

---

### What Bari Must Never Become

**Activist.** Bari is not campaigning against any manufacturer, ingredient, or product category. The moment Bari's findings start to feel like advocacy, credibility has been compromised.

**Influencer.** Bari does not recommend, endorse, or build emotional relationships with products. Influencers build parasocial authority. Bari builds analytical authority.

**Fear-based.** Fear is a powerful editorial tool that Bari explicitly refuses. Alarm language, warning icons, urgent copy — these belong to a media register that trades on anxiety. Bari trades on information.

**Wellness guru.** The wellness media complex tells people what is "good for them" as if that is a universal, context-free judgment. Bari does not know what is good for you. It knows what is in the product.

**Recommendation engine.** Ratings platforms recommend. Review platforms recommend. Bari interprets. The moment Bari starts telling people what to buy, it has become a different product — and a less trustworthy one.

**Pseudo-scientific.** Internal rigor without consumer-facing science theater. Bari does not use technical language to perform credibility. Bari uses plain language to communicate findings. The engine can be sophisticated. The output should be clear.

---

### The Identity Test

Read any Bari output and ask: who does this sound like?

It should sound like: a careful analyst who has read every label in the category and is telling you exactly what they found.

It should not sound like: a nutritionist, a food blogger, a regulator, a lawyer, or a brand.

The analyst voice is calm, specific, bounded, and honest. It is interested in what is there — not in what should be there, what was promised, or what would be better.

That is the voice Bari is building toward. That is the voice that earns long-term trust.

## Known Framework Distortions Registry

This section documents confirmed architectural limitations that are understood, bounded, and deferred to a future BSIP release. Entries here are not bugs — they are deliberate deferral decisions with full audit trails.

Each entry follows the format: ID / title / problem / evidence / decision / BSIP3 requirements / status.

---

### DISTORTION-001 — Category-blind fiber expectation penalizes naturally fiber-free dairy

**Date logged:** 2026-05-29  
**Status:** Documented. Not implemented. Deferred to BSIP3.  
**Owner:** BSIP3 scoring architecture  

---

#### 1. Problem Statement

The `score_nutrient_density` function in `score_engine.py` computes:

```
nutrient_density = 0.65 × protein_score + 0.35 × fiber_score
```

The fiber component carries a fixed 35% weight. For products where `dietary_fiber_g` is null or zero, `fiber_score = 0` and the 35% weight is not redistributed — it simply disappears from the score.

Dairy products (milk, yogurt, cheese, fermented dairy) are biologically incapable of containing dietary fiber. This is not a product quality failure — it is a category property. Under the current model, every dairy product loses exactly `0.35 × protein_score` points on the nutrient_density dimension regardless of how high its protein is or how clean its formulation is. This loss propagates to the final score at `0.35 × protein_score × 0.15` points.

The formula was designed for cross-category use. It is correct for bread, snack bars, and plant-based products where fiber is achievable. Applied unchanged to dairy, it introduces a structural downward bias that is independent of product quality.

---

#### 2. GO מועשר בחלבון — Case Study

Product: יופלה GO מועשר בחלבון. Barcode: 7290110321031.  
Profile: 10g protein / 100g, 72 kcal, 0 additives, 3 ingredients (all dairy), NOVA 3.

Under the current model:
- protein_score = 50.0 (for 10g, per breakpoint table)
- fiber_score = 0.0 (fiber is null; dairy has no fiber)
- nutrient_density = 0.65 × 50 + 0.35 × 0 = **32.5**

Under a fiber-normalized model (fiber weight redistributed to protein for intrinsically fiber-free categories):
- nutrient_density = protein_score = **50.0**
- Final score impact: +2.62 points (69.6 → 72.2)

The product scores 70/B currently. The fiber penalty costs it 2.62 final score points for being dairy — not for any formulation weakness.

Fiber penalty by protein level (final score impact, dairy with 0 fiber):

| Protein | Final score lost |
|---------|-----------------|
| 3.2g (plain yogurt) | 0.84 pts |
| 8.5g (Greek yogurt) | 2.23 pts |
| 10.0g (GO מועשר) | 2.62 pts |
| 11.0g (Skyr) | 2.83 pts |
| 12.0g (high-protein cheese) | 3.04 pts |
| 20.0g (protein concentrate) | 4.46 pts |

The penalty scales with protein content, creating a perverse outcome: the more nutritionally concentrated a dairy product is in protein, the more it is penalized for not also containing fiber — something dairy cannot biologically provide.

---

#### 3. Why This Is Not a מעדנים-Only Issue

The distortion affects every category where dairy is the primary substrate:

- **מעדנים:** All 90 products are dairy or dairy-adjacent. 13 of the top 20 receive the systematic downward adjustment.
- **Yogurt system:** All plain and Greek yogurt products are affected. Any planned Skyr products would face the largest penalty in the portfolio (11g+ protein, 0 fiber).
- **Milk and alternatives:** Cow's milk (3.2g protein), hard cheeses (12g+), and soft cheeses are all affected. Plant-based alternatives that contain fiber (soy, oat, almond) are not, creating a structural advantage for plant alternatives on this dimension that is not earned on protein merit.
- **Future categories:** Any category anchored in dairy (cottage cheese, labneh, kefir) will carry this distortion forward.

The distortion is not a category exception — it is a framework-wide bias against any food group that is structurally fiber-free.

---

#### 4. Why Hot Patch Is Rejected

Four reasons:

**1. Cross-category validation required.** The trigger for fiber normalization must correctly identify "intrinsically fiber-free" vs. "fiber-absent due to poor formulation" vs. "fiber data simply missing." In bread and snack bars, fiber=0 or fiber=null is a negative signal (whole grain bread should have fiber; if it doesn't, the score should reflect that). A hot patch applied only to מעדנים would create category-inconsistent behavior and could accidentally exempt products that should be penalized.

**2. Grade changes at boundary.** The audit identified 3 D→C grade changes in the top 20 מעדנים under the normalized model (GO דובדבן 0.7%, באדי תות שדה, פודינג אינסטנט שוקולד). Grade changes require governance review before implementation — they are consumer-facing and affect product ranking.

**3. Trace logging gap.** The current BSIP2 trace does not record whether the fiber normalization trigger fired. An auditable implementation requires a `fiber_normalization_applied: bool` field in the trace so the distortion correction is visible and reversible.

**4. Architectural precedent.** This is the first proposed category-conditional override of a universal scoring formula. Implementing it without a full design document risks establishing an informal exception pattern that future contributors will extend inconsistently.

---

#### 5. Required BSIP3 Validation Before Implementation

Before this correction can ship, all five gates must pass:

**Gate 1 — Cross-category regression.**  
Run the fiber normalization trigger across all scored categories: מעדנים, לחם, חטיפים, חלב ותחליפים, דגני בוקר. Confirm that bread and snack bar products with legitimately low fiber do NOT receive the normalization (they should still be penalized for absent fiber). Confirm that dairy products across all categories receive it consistently.

**Gate 2 — Plant-based boundary test.**  
Confirm the `has_plant_base()` trigger correctly separates:  
- Pure dairy (normalize): milk, yogurt, cheese, labneh, kefir  
- Plant-based dairy alternatives (do not normalize): soy, oat, almond, coconut  
- Mixed products (do not normalize): yogurt with oat added, dairy with inulin fiber added  

Edge case: a dairy product with added inulin (e.g., Danone Activia) genuinely has fiber from the inulin addition and should not be normalized.

**Gate 3 — Grade change audit.**  
For every product that changes grade under the normalized model, prepare a rationale document confirming the new grade is more accurate, not simply higher. Grade changes must be explainable to consumers without referencing the internal formula change.

**Gate 4 — Trace field addition.**  
Add `fiber_normalization_applied: bool` and `fiber_normalization_reason: str` to the BSIP2 trace schema before the first production run. This field must be present even when the normalization does not fire (value: `false`).

**Gate 5 — Governance review.**  
This document must be updated from "Deferred to BSIP3" to "Implemented in BSIP3 — version X" by the BSIP3 architecture owner before the corrected scores are published. No silent implementation.

---

#### 6. Current Status

**Documented. Not implemented.**

The current BSIP2 scores for all dairy products carry the fiber penalty described above. This is a known limitation. The scores are still defensible — the penalty is bounded (max 4.46 pts), no top-tier grade changes result at current protein levels in the מעדנים dataset, and the within-category ranking order is preserved.

Consumer-facing content should not reference this distortion. It is an internal framework limitation, not a product finding.
