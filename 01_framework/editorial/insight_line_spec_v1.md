# Bari Insight-Line Editorial Specification — v1

**Status:** Canonical  
**Date:** 2026-05-28  
**Scope:** All product insight lines across all Bari categories

---

## What an Insight Line Is

An insight line is a single Hebrew sentence — maximum 12 words — that converts one specific, observable fact about a product into a shelf-level observation.

It is:

> What a sharp, curious friend would notice while standing in the supermarket aisle, holding the product.

It is not:

> What a nutritionist would say to warn you. What the framework found. What the score means.

The line sits in the product row, beneath the product name, beside the score chip. It is the most important editorial object on the page — it is what turns a number into a moment of recognition.

---

## The Three Types

Every valid insight line belongs to one of three types. Know which type you are writing.

### Type 1 — Composition Fact

A specific number or ingredient observable directly from the label or nutrition panel.

The structure: state what is there, in what quantity or order.

**Good examples:**
- "10 גרם חלבון ל-100 גרם"
- "הסוכר — הרכיב השני ברשימה"
- "5 רכיבים בלבד"
- "18.5 גרם סיבים ל-100 גרם"
- "קרגינן, פוספאט, קסנטאן — שלושה מייצבים ברשימה"
- "26% פחות סוכר, 1.5 נקודות הפרש מהגרסה הרגילה"

The last example is the best form of Type 1: two numbers side by side that create the contradiction without a word of judgment.

**Weak forms of Type 1:**
- "כמות חלבון גבוהה" — vague, no number
- "סוכר כרכיב עיקרי" — interpretive (what is "עיקרי"?)
- "הרבה תוספים" — not countable, not specific

**Rule:** If you cannot attach a number, ingredient name, or ingredient position to the fact, it is not a Type 1 line. Either find the number or switch to a different type.

---

### Type 2 — Contradiction

When something about the product doesn't fit its positioning. Named directly, without moral commentary.

The structure: state the two things that don't fit. Stop before explaining why they don't fit.

**Good examples:**
- "גרסת הדיאט עם יותר תוספים מהגרסה הרגילה"
- "מוצר החלבון עם רשימת הרכיבים הארוכה ביותר בקטגוריה"
- "לחם שאור — שמרים תעשייתיים ברשימת הרכיבים"
- "26% פחות סוכר, ציון זהה לגרסה הרגילה"
- "הגביע הכי מוכר בקטגוריה, הציון הנמוך בה"

**What makes them good:** The contradiction is named, not judged. The reader resolves the dissonance. The line does not say "this is misleading" or "this is wrong" — it names two things that are simultaneously true.

**Weak forms of Type 2:**
- "מוצר מטעה" — accusatory, not observational
- "תווית שאינה מגובה" — interpretive verdict
- "מוצר שלא מגיע לציפיות" — the writer is judging the product, not observing it

**Rule:** If the line contains the word "מטעה", "שקר", "לא מגיע", "אינו" in an evaluative sense, or "כי" followed by an explanation — rewrite. Stop at the contradiction. Let it breathe.

**Sharpness boundary:**

> "גרסת הדיאט עם יותר תוספים מהגרסה הרגילה" — WITHIN BOUNDS  
> "גרסת הדיאט עם יותר תוספים — לכן אינה בריאה יותר" — CROSSES LINE

The second crosses the line because "לכן אינה בריאה יותר" is a conclusion the writer is drawing for the reader. The first lets the reader draw it.

---

### Type 3 — Position Fact

Shelf status, relative standing, or categorical rarity. A fact about where this product sits among its peers.

**Good examples:**
- "הגביע הכי מוכר בקטגוריה"
- "אחד מ-3 מוצרים ללא ממתיקים מלאכותיים"
- "הציון הנמוך ביותר בין מוצרי הפודינג"
- "ציון גבוה מרוב מוצרי החלב בקטגוריה — ואין בו חלב"
- "הלחם עם כמות הסיבים הגבוהה ביותר בקבוצה"

**Rule:** The position claim must be verifiable from the dataset. Do not write "הכי טוב" or "הכי גרוע" — those are verdicts. Write "הציון הגבוה ביותר בקטגוריה" or "הציון הנמוך ביותר בין מוצרי X" — those are observable positions.

## Tone Rules

### Approved tone: restrained but fearless

Sharp observations are good. Contradictions are good. Uncomfortable findings are good. Direct wording is good.

The line does not soften uncomfortable findings to avoid discomfort. If the data shows that the most famous product on the shelf scores lowest, the insight line says so.

**Fearless:**
> "הגביע הכי מוכר בקטגוריה, הציון הנמוך ביותר בה"

**Falsely softened (wrong):**
> "מוצר מוכר עם ציון נמוך יחסית"

The word "יחסית" is a retreat from the finding. "הנמוך ביותר" names what is true.

### Forbidden tone: accusatory, moralizing, prescriptive

These words or constructions are not permitted:

| Forbidden | Permitted alternative |
|---|---|
| "מוצר לא בריא" | Specific observation (sugar amount, additive count) |
| "כדאי להימנע" | Nothing — the line does not prescribe |
| "מטעה" | "תווית X, רכיב Y" — name the gap |
| "גרוע" | Specific finding |
| "מצוין" | Specific finding |
| "הציון נמוך בגלל..." | Stop before the explanation |
| "איכות נמוכה" | Specific ingredient or composition observation |
| "לא מומלץ" | Nothing — the line does not recommend |
| "בריא יותר / פחות" | "ציון גבוה / נמוך יותר" + specific reason if needed |

The test: after writing the line, ask — does this line tell the user what to feel, think, or do? If yes, cut that part.

## Structural Rules

**Length:** Maximum 12 Hebrew words. Prefer 7–9. Shorter lines with specific content outperform longer lines with vague content.

**Language:** Conversational Hebrew. Not clinical, not institutional. "5 רכיבים בלבד" not "מוצר המורכב מ-5 מרכיבים". "הסוכר — הרכיב השני" not "הסוכר מדורג שני ברשימת הרכיבים".

**Specificity:** A line is specific if it contains at least one of: a number, an ingredient name, a position count, a comparison to another named product or category average.

**One fact per line:** Do not pack two unrelated facts into a single line. "5 רכיבים ו-10 גרם חלבון" is two facts. Choose the one that creates more meaning for this specific product in this specific context.

**Voice:** Active, declarative. No questions. No ellipsis. No exclamation marks.

## Writing Rules

### Rule 1: Start with what you observed, not how you feel about it

Write what is in the ingredient list, the nutrition panel, or the score. Not what you concluded about what is in the list.

### Rule 2: The number is the editorial

If you have a number that surprises, use it. "26% פחות סוכר, 1.5 נקודות הפרש" is better than "הפחתת הסוכר לא שינתה את הציון" because the numbers do the work and the reader does the interpretation.

### Rule 3: Name the contradiction at its highest specificity

"גרסת הדיאט עם יותר תוספים מהגרסה הרגילה" is better than "גרסת הדיאט עם רכיבים שונים" because "יותר תוספים" names the direction of the contradiction.

"מוצר החלבון עם הציון הנמוך ביותר בין מוצרי החלבון" is better than "מוצר חלבון עם ציון לא גבוה" because the first names the exact position.

### Rule 4: Stop before the explanation

The last word of the insight line should be a fact, not a conclusion. Test: if you have written "לכן", "כי", "משמע", "ולכן" — the line has crossed into explanation. Cut at the last fact before the causal word.

### Rule 5: One product, one line

Do not write a line that is only meaningful if you know other products in the category. "הגביע הכי מוכר בקטגוריה" works standalone. "ציון גבוה מ-מילקי" only works if the user knows מילקי. In a product that is compared to מילקי specifically in the highlighted pair, the comparison may be used. In standalone product rows, use standalone facts.

Exception: Type 3 lines often reference relative standing — "אחד מ-3 מוצרים ללא ממתיקים" is acceptable because it is verifiable and names the rarity rather than naming a specific competing product.

## The Grammar Test

Before publishing any insight line, run this test:

1. Does the line contain a specific number, ingredient name, or countable fact? **If no: rewrite.**
2. Can you add "כדאי להימנע" to the end without it sounding wrong? **If yes: cut the part that enabled it.**
3. Does the line use any forbidden word from the tone table? **If yes: remove it.**
4. Is the line 12 words or fewer? **If no: cut.**
5. Does the line name only what is observable, not what it means? **If it names a conclusion: cut the conclusion.**

## Contradiction Handling

When a product exhibits a contradiction between label claim and composition, the insight line should name the contradiction at the most specific level possible.

**General contradiction (weak):** "מוצר הדיאט עם ציון נמוך"  
**Specific contradiction (strong):** "גרסת הדיאט עם ממתיקים מלאכותיים ויותר תוספים מהגרסה הרגילה"

When both facts in the contradiction are strongly specific, the line can carry two clauses. "26% פחות סוכר, 1.5 נקודות הפרש מהגרסה הרגילה" is 10 words and contains two specific numbers — both are needed to name the contradiction fully.

When a product's name contains a claim that the composition does not support, the structure is:
> "[claim from name], [what is actually in the composition]"

Examples:
- "לחם שאור — שמרים תעשייתיים ברשימת הרכיבים" ✓
- "מוצר חלבון — ממתיקים כרכיב השלישי" ✓
- "ללא סוכר — עם 3 ממתיקים מלאכותיים" ✓

## Rhythm Rules

Contrast creates sharpness. Not every product requires a strong contradiction line. Quiet factual lines are necessary for rhythm. If every line is sharp, none of them are.

A product row with a T1 line — "5 רכיבים בלבד" or "הסוכר — הרכיב השני" — is not a failure. It is doing its job: it is the quiet note that makes the next sharp contradiction audible.

**The boring line earns its place.** A set of 60 insight lines where 30 are T2 contradictions will feel prosecutorial. The user is not being investigated — the shelf is being described. Most products are not exceptions. Most products are ordinary. Saying so, precisely, is the editorial discipline.

**Rhythm guidelines:**
- After two consecutive T2 lines, the next line should be T1 or T3 — regardless of how interesting the product is
- T3 position lines can serve as neutral transitions between clusters without feeling flat
- The quietest line in a cluster can be the one that earns the most trust — the user feels that the category is being described honestly, not assembled for maximum effect

**The test for rhythm:** Read the set aloud. If every line creates urgency, something is wrong. The set should have peaks, plains, and the occasional sharp drop — like a good piece of music, not a siren.

## Examples: Excellent vs. Weak

| Product context | Weak line | Strong line |
|---|---|---|
| Most famous product, lowest score in category | "מוצר לא מיוחד" | "הגביע הכי מוכר בקטגוריה, הציון הנמוך בה" |
| Protein product with additives | "מוצר חלבון עם תוספים" | "תווית חלבון, 11 רכיבים ברשימה" |
| Diet product scoring lower than regular | "מוצר דיאט עם ציון נמוך" | "גרסת הדיאט עם יותר תוספים מהגרסה הרגילה" |
| Reduced-sugar with near-identical score | "הפחתת הסוכר לא שיפרה את הציון" | "26% פחות סוכר, 1.5 נקודות הפרש" |
| High-protein clean product | "מוצר בריא עם הרבה חלבון" | "10 גרם חלבון, 3 רכיבים עיקריים" |
| Soy alternative scoring better | "מוצר צמחי עם ציון גבוה" | "ללא חלב, ציון גבוה ממרבית מוצרי החלב בקטגוריה" |
| Instant powder scoring better than ready cup | "הגרסה הביתית עדיפה" | "להכנה ביתית — ציון גבוה ב-18 נקודות מהגביע המוכן" |
| Sourdough with no fermentation detected | "לחם שאור עם בעיות" | "שאור בשם, שמרים תעשייתיים ברשימת הרכיבים" |
| Highest-scoring product in category | "המוצר הטוב ביותר" | "הציון הגבוה ביותר בקטגוריה — מחמצת, קמח מלא, 18.5 גרם סיבים" |
| Product with collagen claim | "מוצר עם קולגן" | "קולגן בתווית, כמות לא מצוינת" |

## Category Translation

Insight lines must use consumer-facing language, not framework terms.

| Internal concept | Insight line language |
|---|---|
| NOVA4 | "מוצר מעובד" (use sparingly) or name specific additives |
| additive_marker_count = 4 | "4 תוספים מזוניים ברשימה" |
| sweetener_detected | "ממתיק מלאכותי" |
| fermentation_detected = false | "שמרים תעשייתיים ברשימת הרכיבים" |
| high protein_quality | "10 גרם חלבון" (number only) |
| binding_cap = 68 | never mentioned |
| structural_class F | never mentioned |
| ingredient_count = 12 | "12 רכיבים ברשימה" |

## Applying the Spec Across Categories

The spec is category-independent. The ingredient vocabulary and position claims differ by category; the rules do not.

For each new category:
1. Identify the 3 most editorially significant products (highest score surprise, strongest contradiction, clearest rarity)
2. Write Type 2 lines for the surprises and contradictions first — these are the most memorable
3. Fill remaining products with Type 1 lines (composition facts)
4. Use Type 3 (position) for the extremes: highest score, lowest score, and category rarities
5. Run grammar test on all lines before publishing

*This spec governs all insight lines across Bari. It does not govern hero sentences, prologue text, driver lines, or methodology text — those have separate rules in `comparison-template-standard-v1.md`.*
