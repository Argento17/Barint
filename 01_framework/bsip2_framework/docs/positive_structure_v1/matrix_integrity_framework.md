# Matrix Integrity Framework

**Status:** Design document — conceptual phase. No formulas or scoring thresholds are defined here.  
**Builds on:** `positive_architecture_framework.md` (food matrix integrity concept), `ingredient_fragmentation_concept.md` (fragmentation spectrum)  
**Purpose:** Establish Bari's first rigorous framework for food matrix integrity as a property independent of NOVA classification.

---

## What the food matrix is

A food's matrix is the physical structural system in which its nutritional components exist. It is not a nutrient. It is not a macronutrient ratio. It is the three-dimensional biological architecture — cell walls, fibrous networks, lipid membranes, protein structures, and water-binding systems — within which fat, protein, carbohydrate, fiber, and micronutrients are co-located and co-delivered.

The matrix is not preserved by listing the right ingredients. It is preserved by not destroying it during production. Two products can declare identical macro profiles from similar-sounding ingredients and have radically different matrices: one intact, one reconstructed from industrial fractions.

This distinction is the entire purpose of this framework.

---

## Why NOVA is an incomplete proxy for matrix integrity

NOVA is a population-level epidemiological tool. It classifies products into four bins based on the nature and extent of processing. It correlates meaningfully with health outcomes at the population level because the things that tend to destroy food matrices — industrial fractional extraction, extrusion, additive-heavy palatability engineering — also tend to be associated with NOVA 3 and NOVA 4.

But NOVA and matrix integrity are not the same dimension. Their divergence is systematic, not occasional:

**NOVA 3, high matrix integrity:** A traditionally baked bread using stone-ground whole grain flour, water, salt, and fermentation starter. Processing is present; the grain matrix is substantially preserved. The fiber, starch, and protein remain in their grain-context relationship. The product would fail a strict NOVA 2 test because of the baking complexity, but its matrix is far more intact than most NOVA 3 products.

**NOVA 3, low matrix integrity:** A "granola protein bar" using oat protein concentrate, pea protein concentrate, date syrup, palm oil, and natural flavors. NOVA 3 classification based on relatively few additives. But every primary ingredient is a fractional extraction from its source food: the oat protein concentrate has had fiber and starch removed; the pea protein concentrate has had fiber discarded; the date syrup has had fiber and water stripped to produce a refined sugar analog. The matrix of oats, peas, and dates is entirely absent. What remains is a reconstruction of macronutrient targets from isolated components.

**NOVA 2, moderate matrix integrity:** Cold-pressed sunflower oil. NOVA 2 (single-ingredient pressed oil). The food matrix of the sunflower seed is entirely gone — oil is, by definition, a fractional extraction of fat from a whole food. Matrix integrity is low despite the clean NOVA classification.

**NOVA 4, moderate matrix integrity:** A bread made with wheat flour, water, yeast, salt, and a small amount of calcium propionate (preservative). NOVA 4 classification due to the preservative. But the bread matrix — the gluten network, the starch-protein relationship, the bread structure — is substantially intact. The calcium propionate does not destroy the food matrix; it prevents spoilage.

The implication for BSIP2: using NOVA alone to assess matrix integrity will systematically mis-evaluate products in both directions — crediting simple extractions (pressed oils) and penalising preserved-matrix products (preserved breads, enzyme-treated whole-grain products).

## The matrix integrity spectrum

Matrix integrity exists on a spectrum anchored by two poles: **intact biological structure** and **reconstructed macronutrient formula**.

```
Intact ─────────────────────────────────────────── Reconstructed
  │                                                      │
Cell walls     Mechanical    Fractional     Molecular     Formula
intact         disruption    extraction     reassembly    from
               minor         single dim.    multi-source  pure fractions
```

Each position on this spectrum has diagnostic properties:

**Intact matrix**
- Nutritional components are physically co-located as they exist in the source food
- Digestion requires active physical breakdown (chewing, stomach acid, enzymatic work)
- Nutrients enter the bloodstream in a sequence mediated by the structural complexity of the food
- The food's caloric density corresponds to its inherent compositional density, not engineering

**Mechanically transformed matrix**
- Physical structure is disrupted but chemical relationships are largely preserved
- Components remain co-present: grinding almond into almond butter disrupts cell walls but fat, protein, and fiber remain in physical proximity
- Digestive rate is accelerated compared to intact, but structural context is maintained
- The nutritional label still approximately reflects the food's inherent composition

**Fractionally extracted matrix**
- One or two components are separated from the rest and concentrated
- The structural relationship between components is severed: oat protein isolate contains protein, but the fiber it formerly co-existed with has been discarded; the beta-glucan that surrounded and interacted with the oat protein is absent
- The extracted component enters a food product without its natural co-factors
- Nutritional labels show this component in a concentration that could not exist in any whole food

**Reconstructed matrix**
- Multiple fractional extractions from multiple source foods are combined to create a target nutritional profile
- No original food matrix is present; the product is a formula
- The resulting texture, structure, and taste are engineered independently from the nutritional composition
- The food matrix is an artefact of industrial assembly, not of biological growth

## Product case studies

The following case studies place specific product types on the matrix integrity spectrum. These are conceptual; the actual score assigned by BSIP2 depends on product-level data.

---

### Almonds (raw or dry-roasted)

**Matrix status:** Intact or minimally disrupted  
**What the matrix contains:** Fat in intact lipid bodies within almond cells; protein embedded in almond tissue; fiber (the cell wall structure itself); vitamin E co-located with the fat; magnesium and calcium within the cell structure

**Metabolic consequence of intact matrix:** Studies consistently show that whole almonds deliver fewer digestible calories than the label suggests because the cell wall structure resists complete digestion — approximately 20-25% of the fat in intact almonds passes through the gut without absorption. This is a function of the intact matrix, not the nutrient content. Ground almond meal at the same macro profile delivers more absorbed calories.

**NOVA classification:** NOVA 1 (raw) or NOVA 2 (dry-roasted)  
**Matrix integrity assessment:** Very high  
**Divergence from NOVA:** None significant — NOVA correctly identifies this as minimally processed

---

### Rolled oats vs. extruded oat puffs

**Rolled oats matrix status:** Mechanically transformed but largely intact  
The steaming and rolling process disrupts the outer structure but does not extract components. Beta-glucan fiber remains embedded in the grain. Protein and fat remain co-located in the kernel. The glycemic behaviour is slower than oat flour because the matrix still partially resists rapid enzymatic digestion.

**Extruded oat puff matrix status:** Reconstructed  
Even when made from whole oat flour (which is already a step removed from whole oats), extrusion under heat and pressure gelatinises the starch completely, destroys the protein network, eliminates the fibrous cell wall structures, and produces a highly porous, rapidly digestible product. The same beta-glucan that provides rolled oats' glycemic moderation effect is broken down during extrusion into a form that provides reduced viscosity in digestion. The puffed product digests rapidly despite having "oats" as its primary ingredient.

**Critical BSIP2 implication:** The signal "contains oats" or "whole grain oats" in an ingredient list is an L1 observation. Matrix integrity is an L3 inference. The word "oats" appears in both products; the matrix is present in one and absent in the other. Signal detection must distinguish the ingredient form, not just the ingredient name.

---

### Greek yogurt

**Matrix status:** Transformed but structurally coherent  
Greek yogurt is made by straining whey from fermented milk, concentrating the milk proteins (predominantly casein) to 2–3× their original density. The fermentation process partially pre-digests the proteins into smaller peptides. The fat (in full-fat versions) remains in its milk fat globule structure.

**Fermentation note:** Fermentation is a specific form of processing that can enhance matrix properties. The lactic acid bacteria produce organic acids and enzymes that: (a) reduce lactose, improving tolerability; (b) partially pre-digest proteins, improving digestibility; (c) produce bioactive peptides with functional properties. Greek yogurt is structurally transformed but nutritionally enhanced by the transformation — this is the paradigm case for beneficial matrix alteration.

**NOVA classification:** NOVA 1–2  
**Matrix integrity assessment:** High, despite significant processing  
**Key principle:** Matrix integrity is not preserved by doing nothing. It is determined by what the process does to the structural relationship between the food's components. Greek yogurt's straining concentrates the protein-fat-culture matrix without destroying it; extrusion destroys the original matrix and replaces it with an industrial structure.

### Peanut butter (natural vs. commercial)

**Natural peanut butter (peanuts + salt):**  
Matrix status: Mechanically transformed, intact coherence  
Grinding peanuts disrupts cell walls and releases natural oil, creating a paste. The protein, fat, and fiber of the peanut remain in physical proximity. The nutritional properties approximate ground whole peanuts. The oil that rises to the top (natural separation) is evidence of the intact, non-emulsified structure — no industrial process has been applied to keep it homogeneous.

**Commercial peanut butter (peanuts, sugar, palm oil, mono- and diglycerides, salt):**  
Matrix status: Reconstructed  
The natural peanut oil has been removed (or at least supplemented) with palm oil; mono- and diglycerides are added to prevent oil separation (emulsification engineering); sugar is added for palatability. The product maintains a peanut-matrix appearance but components have been substituted and the natural structural relationships engineered away. This is a modest form of reconstruction — not as extreme as a protein bar — but the matrix coherence is lower.

**Scoring implication:** Two products labeled "peanut butter" can have different matrix integrity. The ingredient list is the distinguishing signal: peanuts + salt = intact coherence; peanuts + palm oil + emulsifiers = partial reconstruction.

---

### Hummus

**Matrix status:** Mechanically transformed, mostly intact  
Cooked chickpeas ground with tahini (ground sesame), lemon juice, garlic, and salt. The chickpea matrix — starch, protein, fiber — is ground and made more bioavailable by cooking (which denatures lectins and reduces phytates). The tahini brings sesame's fat matrix. The result is a food with high structural coherence: the chickpea protein arrives with the chickpea fiber; the sesame fat arrives with sesame's natural fat structure.

**Notable:** Hummus includes beneficial processing (cooking chickpeas for lectin reduction and phytate reduction via heat and, often, acidification). The matrix transformation is in the direction of nutritional improvement.

**Matrix integrity assessment:** High  
**Comparison with protein bars:** Hummus has a protein profile similar to some protein bars (~5g/100g). But the protein in hummus arrives with 6-7g of fiber per 100g, a natural fat structure, and minimal added ingredients. The same protein quantity in a protein bar arrives as an isolate, without fiber context, surrounded by other isolated components.

### Protein isolate bar

**Matrix status:** Reconstructed formula  
A typical protein bar might contain: whey protein isolate (protein extracted from whey, all fat and lactose removed), chicory root fiber (inulin extracted from chicory root, a different food entirely), palm kernel oil (fat extracted from palm kernel), cocoa powder (fat partially extracted), erythritol or maltitol (sugar alcohol), flavoring, lecithin (emulsifier from soy or sunflower).

Each component is a fraction of a different source food. No single source food's matrix is intact or even partially present. The protein is from dairy; the fiber is from chicory root; the fat is from palm. They have been assembled to produce a target macro profile (high protein, moderate fat, low net carb). The macronutrient label is accurate; the food matrix is entirely absent.

**Why this matters for Bari:** A protein isolate bar can score well in the current BSIP2 architecture if its sugar is low, its fiber is high (from added inulin), its protein is high (from isolate), and its additive count is modest. The current system cannot distinguish this from a food that delivers the same macros through an intact matrix — because it is measuring components, not structure.

**Gaming vulnerability:** This is precisely the architecture that sophisticated food engineering can optimize: find the macro ratios BSIP2 rewards, assemble them from isolated components, maintain a short-ish ingredient list of clean-sounding names. The product "wins" the score without delivering the structural quality the score was intended to represent.

---

### Extruded breakfast cereal

**Matrix status:** Reconstructed  
Refined grain flour (starch fraction) is processed under high heat and pressure through an extruder, forming a puffed, expanded structure. The output has no relationship to the source grain's structure. The protein network of the grain has been denatured; the starch is fully gelatinized and rapidly digestible; the fiber (often absent from the refined flour input, or minimal) does not reconstruct the original grain's fibrous architecture. Vitamins and minerals are often sprayed on after extrusion to remediate losses from the process.

**The "whole grain" problem:** Some extruded cereals use whole grain flour as input. This generates a "whole grain" claim and activates the `has_whole_grain` positive signal in BSIP2's current architecture. But extrusion through whole grain flour does not produce a whole grain product. The structural properties of the grain — slow starch digestion, intact fiber networks, protein-fiber co-location — have been destroyed by the extrusion process regardless of the input flour type. "Whole grain" as an ingredient claim and "whole grain matrix intact" are different things.

---

### Diet mousse / low-calorie aerated dessert

**Matrix status:** Engineered structural simulation  
A diet mousse at 40–70 kcal/100g typically contains: skim milk, modified corn starch, thickeners (carrageenan, xanthan gum, locust bean gum), artificial sweeteners, flavorings, and sufficient air incorporation to create apparent volume. The structural experience of the food — its mousse-like consistency, its apparent satiety — is engineered from hydrocolloid systems and air incorporation. The caloric content is low because most of the volume is air and water thickened by extracted polysaccharides.

**The relevant concern:** A diet mousse can avoid many BSIP2 penalties: sugar is low (sweeteners), fat is low, calories are low, sodium may be low. If the NOVA inference misses its sweetener signals, it can appear to be a fairly clean product. But the satiety it delivers is largely structural illusion — air and thickeners — and the food contributes essentially no meaningful nourishment. It is the purest example of a product that scores well by absence-of-penalties logic while delivering structural absence.

**Matrix integrity assessment:** Near zero — there is no food matrix to speak of. The structural properties of the product are engineering artefacts.

## Dimensions of matrix integrity

From the case studies, five distinct dimensions emerge:

**1. Component co-location**  
Are the macro- and micronutrients of the primary ingredients still in physical proximity to one another, as they were in the source food? Fat and fiber co-located in the almond cell = high co-location. Whey protein isolate (fat removed) mixed with chicory fiber (from a different food) = low co-location.

**2. Structural complexity resistance**  
Does the food's structure resist rapid enzymatic digestion? Intact almond cell walls slow fat absorption. Puffed extruded starch is instantaneously available to digestive enzymes. This is the mechanism behind the intact matrix's glycemic and satiety advantages.

**3. Transformation coherence**  
Was the transformation applied to the food (if any) coherent with the food's own nature — concentration, fermentation, grinding — or was it structural destruction and reconstruction? Greek yogurt: fermentation coherent with dairy. Protein bar: reconstruction from fractions of multiple foods.

**4. Ingredient declaration-to-matrix correspondence**  
Does the macro profile declared on the label plausibly arise from the structural ingredients declared? If a product claims 25g protein per 100g and its ingredients are oat groats, dates, and cashews (no isolates), this is plausible — but unusual and would require verification. If a product claims 25g protein from those same ingredients without any isolates, it is likely a misdeclaration or enrichment not shown in ingredients. If it lists whey isolate, the source is explained.

**5. Process reversibility**  
Could the transformation process be partially reversed? Ground almonds could notionally be reconstituted; extruded puffs could not. Yogurt fermentation could not be reversed, but the matrix properties of the cultured milk protein remain. This dimension distinguishes structural transformation from structural destruction.

## What matrix integrity is not

**It is not "unprocessed equals good."** Cold-pressed palm oil is NOVA 2, single-ingredient, minimally processed — and represents the fractional extraction of fat from a whole food. Its matrix integrity score would be low: it is an extracted fraction. Raw unprocessed lard is NOVA 1 — same assessment.

**It is not a proxy for NOVA.** As demonstrated: NOVA 3 products can have high matrix integrity; NOVA 2 products can have low matrix integrity. The signals overlap substantially but are not identical.

**It is not a claim that food structure always determines nutritional quality.** Protein isolates can serve legitimate purposes (clinical nutrition, specific athletic needs). Extracted oils have been used in healthy dietary patterns for millennia. The framework asks whether the structural properties of a food are intact — not whether intact structure is mandatory for health.

**It is not a heritage or tradition signal.** A traditionally made food can have low matrix integrity (clarified butter, refined cane sugar from traditional mills). A novel food can have high matrix integrity (a carefully formulated whole-food product from clean ingredients).

## BSIP2 design implications

The existing `ingredient_fragmentation_concept.md` establishes the conceptual vocabulary. This framework adds the following design requirements for any matrix integrity signal in BSIP2:

**Requirement 1: Ingredient-form sensitivity** — ✅ IMPLEMENTED (TASK-133A, 2026-06-01)  
The signal must distinguish ingredient form, not just ingredient presence. "Oats" and "oat protein isolate" are different; "almonds" and "almond protein concentrate" are different. This requires an ingredient taxonomy with fragmentation level metadata.  
*Implemented as `proto_v0/src/ingredient_taxonomy.py`: resolves name/synonym → E-number, additive_class, `fragmentation_level` (intact \| mechanical \| fractional \| reconstructed), and `is_named_concern`. Consumed by Protein Quality (TASK-133B reconstructed/collagen discount), Additive Quality (TASK-133C emulsifier identity), and the BHA named penalty (TASK-133D). Primary-ingredient weighting (Req 3) and gaming resistance (Req 5) are realized by `primary_fragmentation_profile()`.*

**Requirement 2: NOVA independence**  
The matrix integrity assessment must produce results that differ from NOVA assignment for a meaningful fraction of products. If the two are perfectly correlated, matrix integrity adds no information. The target is a signal that is correlated with NOVA at moderate strength but captures variance that NOVA does not.

**Requirement 3: Primary ingredient focus**  
The assessment should weight primary ingredients (by position in the ingredient list and by declared quantity if available) more heavily than minor additives. A product with whole oat groats as the first ingredient and a small amount of an extracted fiber additive is structurally different from a product where the first ingredient is pea protein isolate.

**Requirement 4: Construction-method signal**  
Where process information is available (extrusion is often inferable from product form and texture description; fermentation is declared on pack; mechanical pressing is inferable from "cold-pressed" claims), it should be used to modulate the matrix assessment.

**Requirement 5: Gaming resistance**  
The signal must not be gameable by adding a small amount of a whole-food ingredient and claiming the halo. A product with 95% isolated components and 5% rolled oat addition is not a high-matrix-integrity product. The primary ingredient composition — and the ingredient fragmentation level of those primary ingredients — should determine the assessment.
