## Synthetic Insights: What Held Up in Real Data

- **Fiber laundering** as a pattern: present in real products with isolated-fiber additives
  (inulin/chicory) listed in Hebrew ingredient text.
- **Anchor override** routing (לחם, קרקר, מחמצת) behaves as expected on real product names.
- **Sourdough spectrum** confirmed: genuine מחמצת products exist alongside industrial
  yeast products using the מחמצת label.
- **Product name missingness** is real: some OFF records have only an English name
  (no Hebrew), triggering `product_name_short_no_anchor` appropriately.

## Synthetic Insights: What Did NOT Hold Up / New Findings

- **Coverage gap:** OFF has only ~42 Israeli bread/cracker products with usable data.
  Synthetic corpus covered more sub-types by design. The real market's diversity
  is higher than what OFF records.
- **Ingredient text quality:** Many real products have ingredients in Latin
  transliteration rather than Hebrew. Signal extraction on transliterated text
  is degraded — a gap the synthetic corpus did not model.
- **Nutrition completeness:** 10/42
  real products lack fiber data. Synthetic corpus was 100% complete by design.
  This systematically affects fiber-related scoring and bakery semantics.
- **Crackers gap:** OFF Israeli coverage for crackers is near-zero. The synthetic
  corpus included rich cracker/crispbread subtypes not yet captured from real data.
- **Retailer provenance:** Real data rarely specifies the retailer. OFF stores names
  are inconsistently populated. Synthetic corpus had clean retailer labels.

## Key Reality Test Findings

1. **The 100-product target is unachievable from publicly accessible Israeli retail data** 
   without direct retailer API access. OFF yields ~42 relevant products.
   Israeli retailers actively block scraping (HTTP 403 or maintenance mode).

2. **Missing fiber data is the primary scoring bottleneck.** Synthetic calibration
   assumed fiber was always available. Real products frequently omit it from OFF.

3. **Bakery semantics generalizes well.** Routing (anchor→signal→resolution) works
   correctly on real Hebrew product names. No systematic mis-routes detected.

4. **Confidence calibration is appropriate.** Real products with missing ingredients
   correctly land at CAUTIOUS or INSUFFICIENT. The patch v1 deductions fire
   on real data at expected rates.

5. **Scraper architecture needs direct retailer API access** to reach 100+ products
   in bread/cracker categories. OFF is a useful supplement, not the primary source.
